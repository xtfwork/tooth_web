  • Study reveals major gaps in public understanding of dental team and urgent care access
    Published: 2025-08-12 02:29

    CARDIFF, Wales: A new qualitative study has found that many dental patients in Wales remain unclear about the different roles within National Health Service...

    AI summary

    A new study conducted by Health and Care Research Wales Evidence Centre at Cardiff University reveals that many Welsh residents lack understanding of the roles within NHS dental teams and how to access urgent dental care. The research, involving 44 participants across all seven health boards in Wales, found that while most recognized dentists, dental nurses, and dental hygienists, few were aware of dental technicians or dental therapists. Participants often used the term "dental assistant" for any professional working alongside a dentist, suggesting a lack of awareness about team members' specific training and scope of practice. The confusion extends to urgent dental care pathways, with many unsure about what constitutes a dental emergency, who to contact, or where to go for help. While some said they would call their own dentist or NHS 111, others admitted that they might unnecessarily attend accident and emergency departments. The study's authors suggest that this lack of knowledge could undermine current reforms aimed at encouraging patients to seek care from the most appropriate professional. The participants proposed solutions such as multichannel public awareness campaigns, school-based education, and community outreach, which would clarify the makeup of dental teams and distinguish between routine, urgent, and emergency care. The study titled "Public awareness of care pathways and available skill mix in NHS dental teams: A qualitative study" was published on 11th July 2025 in the British Dental Journal.
