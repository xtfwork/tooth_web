    AI summary

    In an interview with Dentistry Today, experts from HuFriedyGroup emphasize the importance of maintaining dental unit waterlines (DUWLs) for patient safety and staff protection. While DUWLs are essential for cooling and irrigating during dental procedures, they can harbor bacterial growth due to their small size and potential stagnation. Bacteria can form a slimy protective layer of biofilm in the waterlines, which continues to feed and multiply without regular cleaning, disinfection, and monitoring. This biofilm is difficult to remove once established, requiring a "waterline shock treatment" to remove it. Recent years have seen increased attention to waterline safety due to outbreaks linked to contaminated dental waterlines, particularly in pediatric dental offices. Vulnerable patients like children, the elderly, immunocompromised individuals, and even dental staff are at risk of exposure to contaminated dental waterlines. States are starting to enact laws requiring quarterly dental waterline testing, with more likely to follow. Practices should look to their state dental board, CDC, ADA, and ADS for guidance on the best methods for ensuring clean water. The DentaPure Cartridge, a product by HuFriedyGroup, is designed for use on DUWLs attached to dynamic instruments. It contains iodinated resin beads that release 2 to 6ppm of elemental iodine into the water passing through the cartridges, controlling bacterial growth. One cartridge maintains compliant procedural water for 365 days or 240L of water and is easy to install and use. Elemental iodine in the DentaPure Cartridge does not cause sensitivity reactions, and at typical elution rates, exposure during a dental visit is far exceeded by common foods like milk and eggs. The system is simple to use with minimal learning curve. The consistent implementation of waterline maintenance protocols benefits both patients and staff by reducing the risk of infection and ensuring compliance with microbiological standards. Regular testing, shocking, and maintenance are essential for assessing compliance, verifying proper implementation of protocols, and ensuring lines are clean and ready for continued patient treatment.

Autografting, using a patient's own bone for dental implantation and reconstruction, remains the gold standard.
