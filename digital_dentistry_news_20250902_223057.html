    In a large-scale study, researchers from Shiga University of Medical Science found that regular dental maintenance preserves more teeth than treatment-only care, regardless of age or diabetes status. The study, published in Diabetology International, analyzed a Japanese health database covering 705,542 adults aged 20–74. Despite not establishing causality, the study's strength lies in its extensive data scale and detail.
