  • “The Oral Health Observatory has been a goldmine of insights for China”
    Published: 2025-09-09 01:56

    As oral health takes on growing importance within broader public health strategies, data-driven insights are becoming essential for shaping effective policy...

    AI summary

    In an interview with Dental Tribune International, Dr Liu Yi, director of international communication at the Chinese Stomatological Association and clinical professor at Peking University School of Stomatology, discussed China's involvement in FDI's Oral Health Observatory (OHO) project. The initiative aims to standardize oral health data globally by comparing oral health across countries. China joined the project to learn from others' experiences, reduce oral health gaps, and fill data gaps in their own country. The OHO project collects real-time data from dental clinics using a user-friendly app developed by FDI. This approach has provided valuable insights into patient behaviors, clinical outcomes, and oral health trends in China. Key findings include the correlation between poor oral hygiene habits (such as less frequent brushing, smoking, and consuming sugary foods) and worse gingival health and cavities. Additionally, the data revealed that many Chinese patients tend to seek treatment only when issues arise, emphasizing the need for prevention and oral health education. The OHO's data collection method aligns with the World Health Organization's push to integrate oral health into broader health strategies for non-communicable diseases. The app-based approach could offer a more cost-effective solution for oral health monitoring in China, potentially guiding smarter policies and improving clinical care. Over time, this information could make oral health programs more effective and sustainable.
