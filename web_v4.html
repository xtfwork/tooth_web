  • A look at dental X-ray radiation: Safety and recommendations

    Dental X-rays are safe and expose patients to low levels of radiation, even less than what an individual is exposed to in the environment.

    AI Summary

    The article "A look at dental X-ray radiation: Safety and recommendations" by ADA Huddles discusses the safety of dental X-rays and provides recommendations for their use. According to experts, including Erika Benavides, D.D.S., Ph.D., dental professionals should perform clinical examinations before deciding whether X-rays are necessary. Dental X-rays are used to identify oral health issues such as dental caries, cysts, tumors, and gum disease in their early stages, as well as image underlying permanent teeth in children. Despite concerns about ionizing radiation's ability to damage DNA and increase the risk of cancer, dental X-rays expose patients to very low levels of this radiation. They also maintain that dental X-rays are safe for pregnant women and their fetuses, exposing them to levels far lower than the threshold needed to cause harm. The article was published in The Journal of the American Dental Association in 2024. It is important to note that this article does not imply the ADA's endorsement or agreement with its content.
