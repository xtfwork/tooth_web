    AI summary

    The report "Artificial Intelligence and Dental Service Provision: A Rapid Evidence Assessment" highlights both the potential benefits and challenges of incorporating AI in dental care. The study found that while AI can aid diagnostics, it's prone to false negatives for subtle conditions like voids or early caries, underscoring its role as a support tool rather than replacement for human judgment. Patient reactions were mixed; some appreciated robotics-assisted surgery, but others felt anxious due to the complexity of AI machinery. Compliance with AI monitoring tools decreased over time due to repetitive messages or frequent scans causing disengagement. User error in capturing intra-oral photographs also affected reliability. The report revealed a lack of evidence regarding issues like equality, diversity, inclusion, and data protection despite the generation of large patient data volumes by AI systems. The authors suggest UK-specific research to address these concerns within the UK's cultural and social context. In conclusion, the report acknowledges AI's potential in enhancing dental services but emphasizes that its practical application in daily practice is currently limited.
