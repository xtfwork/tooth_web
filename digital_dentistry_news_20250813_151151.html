  • Survey exposes systemic failings behind burn-out in dental teams
    Published: 2025-08-13 04:43

    DUNDEE, Scotland: A landmark UK study has found that stress and burn-out across the dental workforce stem largely from systemic issues, particularly within ...

    AI summary

    The British Dental Journal has published a study highlighting the high levels of stress and burnout among dental professionals in the UK, primarily attributed to systemic issues within the National Health Service (NHS) dentistry. The study, titled "Me, we, they: Identifying the key stressors affecting the dental team", gathered responses from over 1,500 dental professionals. The main stress factors identified were workload, NHS system pressures, regulatory compliance and litigation, financial pressures, leadership and management, and self-worth. An intolerable workload was the most common complaint, worsened by recruitment and retention difficulties. The NHS contractual framework, particularly England's units of dental activity system, was widely criticized for creating unachievable targets and constant pressure. Regulation and litigation fears were also prominent, with dentists expressing concerns about the atmosphere from regulatory bodies and the fear of disciplinaries and litigation. Financial strain was keenly felt, with dental care professionals highlighting poor wages and practice owners struggling with stagnant NHS funding and rising costs. Poor leadership quality, lack of support, and feelings of low self-worth were also common among respondents. The study concludes that current reactive approaches are insufficient and that macro-level change is essential to enable dental teams to develop, flourish, and feel valued. Without such changes, the profession faces worsening burnout, attrition, and risks to patient safety. The findings underscore the urgent need for reform within NHS dentistry to address these systemic issues and improve the mental health and well-being of dental professionals.
