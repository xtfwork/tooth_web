  • Alpha-gal syndrome: Tick-borne allergy poses hidden risks in dentistry
    Published: 2025-08-18 22:49

    BALTIMORE, US: Alpha-gal syndrome is a delayed allergic reaction to a specific carbohydrate molecule found in mammalian meat and certain medical products ...

    AI summary

    In a recent study by researchers from the US, it was discovered that Alpha-gal syndrome, a delayed allergic response to a carbohydrate molecule found in mammalian meat and certain medical products, could pose risks to patients in the dental setting. The condition, which has seen over 110,000 cases in the US between 2010 and 2022, with a significant increase in recent years, can lead to severe allergic reactions such as anaphylaxis when triggered by exposure to red meat, dairy products, or dental products containing similar compounds. The research team examined dental products for potential Alpha-gal triggers, identifying multiple materials and ingredients that could pose risks. They found that dental products like haemostatic solutions, suture materials, toothpastes, artificial saliva substitutes, fluoride gels, soft-tissue and bone grafting materials, tooth prophylaxis pastes, and mouthwashes may contain compounds that could trigger an Alpha-gal reaction. The study emphasized the need for dental professionals to be aware of Alpha-gal syndrome and to carefully review the ingredients of dental products before use in affected patients. They should consult with the patient's allergist or internist when a patient mentions having had this condition, and be prepared to manage emergent outcomes associated with severe allergic reactions. The findings were published online in the July 2025 issue of the Journal of the American Dental Association under the title "Alpha-gal syndrome: Potential for a hypersensitivity reaction after the use of dental products."
