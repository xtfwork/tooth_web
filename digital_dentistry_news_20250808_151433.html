    This article discusses the benefits of polished zirconia crowns over surface-glazed versions in restorative dentistry. Zirconia, a popular material for dental crowns due to its strength, biocompatibility, and esthetics, is vulnerable to wear on opposing enamel when glazed with feldspathic materials. This glazing technique can cause accelerated wear on natural teeth over time due to the roughened surface left by worn glaze, increasing risk of sensitivity, caries, and structural weakness. The article emphasizes that polished zirconia crowns offer long-term oral health benefits by preserving enamel and reducing erosion risk compared to surface-glazed versions.
