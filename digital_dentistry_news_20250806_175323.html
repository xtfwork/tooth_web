    AI summary

    In a recent whitepaper titled "Poverty Shapes Oral Health," DSO Benevis highlighted the connection between socioeconomic status and oral healthcare access, revealing that older, lower-income adults experience triple the amount of untreated tooth decay compared to their higher-income counterparts. Children with public insurance also receive less dental treatment than those with private insurance. Dr. Jane Whang, Regional Director of Clinical at Benevis, discussed these findings and the state of dental care accessibility in the U.S., emphasizing that socioeconomic disparities persist and affect overall health. Misconceptions about oral healthcare include assuming that free care is accessible and fair, as well as overlooking the need for adult dental coverage as people live longer. Solutions to alleviate these disparities proposed by Dr. Whang include stopping cuts to Medicaid, making enrollment processes easier, providing support for more providers to accept Medicaid insurance, and implementing better fee packages to sustain practices amidst increased costs in the dental industry. The biggest concern is that upcoming cuts could lead to fewer providers accepting Medicaid, further decreasing access to care for vulnerable populations.
