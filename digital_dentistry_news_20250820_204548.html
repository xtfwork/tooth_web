    AI summary

    The article discusses a novel technique for bonding auxiliary buttons in aligner therapy, developed by Dr Viet Anh Nguyen, a researcher at Phenikaa University in Hanoi. Unlike conventional methods, this thermal embedding technique eliminates debonding issues and increases chair time efficiency. The method involves softening the aligner polymer using heat from stainless steel buttons, allowing them to be embedded within the aligners. This adhesiveless bond is achieved through mechanical interlocking. The technique has been observed to work mechanically without debonding under standard forces, making it suitable for immediate elastic traction cases such as Class III malocclusion and canine exposure. It serves as a backup for precision cut unavailability situations but should not be used with materials of low thermal resistance. The report notes that more studies are needed to validate the technique, but its mechanical efficacy has been demonstrated. The benefits include increased clinical efficiency due to streamlined visits, reduced costs from eliminating bonding primers and adhesives, and aesthetic advantages for aligner therapy. The technique allows a trained dental assistant to perform the procedure under supervision, helping standardise workflow and free up dentist time. The report was published in the Cureus Journal of Medical Science on July 28, 2025.
