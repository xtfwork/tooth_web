    AI summary

    Dr. Louis Kaufman, a dental consultant and lecturer, shares his experience with a phone scam in July 2025. The caller claimed to be a Highland Park police officer, providing personal details about him, including his date of birth, business address, and federal check information. They claimed he had been overpaid through the ERC program, missed a court hearing, and a warrant was issued for his arrest. The fraudster directed him to withdraw cash using Bitcoin and pay a fine, all while staying on the phone. Despite his suspicions, the scammer's convincing answers and specific details kept Kaufman in doubt. It wasn't until he accessed another phone and called the Lake County Sheriff's Department that he realized the call was fraudulent. Kaufman and his wife reported the incident to the police, who informed them that they were targeted due to their clean criminal record, successful profession, and recent receipt of PPP and ERC funds. The scammers use "ghosting" phone lines, official language, and public records to build credible stories. The experience left Kaufman feeling a complex mix of emotions, including humiliation, anger, relief, and unease. He decided to share his story to prevent others from falling victim to similar scams. His advice is to never provide personal information over the phone or wire money to unknown sources. If contacted by someone claiming to be law enforcement, hang up and report the incident to local police immediately.
