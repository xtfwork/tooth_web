    CHICAGO, US: As one of President Donald Trump’s proudest accomplishments, the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBB), enacted in July 2025, is a sweeping ...

    AI summary

    The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBB), enacted in July 2025, is a sweeping budget reconciliation law that significantly impacts the dental profession. Key provisions include tax benefits, such as the restoration of the pass-through entity tax deduction, a $40,000 cap on the state and local tax deduction for pass-through entities until 2030, and measures that allow practices to invest more in technology and infrastructure. The act also addresses education and workforce issues by making post-secondary credentialing expenses eligible as higher-education costs, benefiting dental students. Additionally, it modifies employer-provided student loan repayment rules to exclude a portion of repayments from taxable income. OBBB overhauls federal student loan programs beginning in July 2026, eliminating Graduate PLUS loans for new borrowers and introducing strict annual and lifetime borrowing caps. Medicaid changes between 2026 and 2028 could reduce adult dental services in some states due to funding adjustments, new co-pay allowances, and stricter eligibility rules. Dentists should consult with tax professionals, financial aid offices, and state dental societies as they adapt their financial planning and advocacy efforts to this complex legislative change.
